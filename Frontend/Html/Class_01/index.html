About Ratan Tata

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He is known for his leadership in the Tata Group, one of India's largest conglomerates.

Born on December 28, 1937, Ratan Tata is the great-grandson of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group. He joined the Tata Group in 1962 and became its chairman in 1991, a position he held until his retirement in 2012.

During his tenure, Ratan Tata oversaw significant growth and diversification of the Tata Group. He played a key role in the launch of several successful ventures, including Tata Motors' introduction of the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car, and the acquisition of international brands like Jaguar Land Rover and Corus Steel.

Overall, Ratan Tata is widely respected for his business acumen, visionary leadership, and commitment to social causes, making him one of India's most influential figures in the corporate world.
